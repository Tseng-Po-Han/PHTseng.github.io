    Practice of Mechanical Engineering, Pneumatic Kart(2019.03~2019.06)

    The juniors of NTUME will design a pneumatic kart in the second semester, with the special to-be-solved problem evolving every year. Our final objective is to make our kart automatically finish an unknown route and, meanwhile, follow another kart with same speed. To achieve this goal, we must design the propeller, the kart frame , and also the Ackerman steering mechanism. Of equal importance, we have to program the control code to recognize the route and detect the front or behind kart, and therefore to adapt the speed or direction. To design comprehensively, tests need to be conduct to analyze the thrust force of propeller, the effectiveness of steering machanism, the stiffness, toughness, and strength of kart frame. Thereby, numerical analysis and experiments, e.g., wind tunnel test, are carried out. In the final competition, we made the second place of the kart relay race.

    Two Phase Flow, Design and Analysis of Condensors(2020.05~2020.06)

    This is a final project of this graduate course. In this project, we are ordered to design a condensor of a cooling system, which shall be able to utilize on the automobile or refrigerating system.The two phase flow in the process of condensation makes the design and analysis of the heat transfer mechanism complicated. Hence, several considerations and efforts are needed. Eventually, given any inlet conditions of the refrigerant, our model are able to derive the total heat transfer and the pressure drop, which is critical for choosing the pump for the cooling system.

    Introduction to Biomedical Engineering, A Novel Study of Remote Percutaneous Coronary Intervention(2020.05~2020.06)

    This project aims to realize the technique of remote PCI.The traditional PCI forced unfortunately the surgeon alternate between two serious disadvantages, the high-level radiation of developer or the hip-bone-attacking protective clothing. Consequently comes the remote PCI. However, since the surgeons operate the wire-passing machine by some buttons or knobs, the realistic feel is deconnected from the surgeons. On the basis of this reason, we target to design a mechanism to connect the surgeon and the passing wire in real-time, which means "sensing" the force feed-back at the front of the wire, and the reproduce the feelings at the end of the surgeon.
